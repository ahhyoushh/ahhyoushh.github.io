Time Budget (~83 sec/question)
Maths ~55 min · Physics ~42 min · Chemistry ~42 min · English+LR ~21 min. English & Reasoning = 90 marks total — most JEE students massively under-prepare for this. Two focused weeks locks in 70+ out of 90 here.
Strategy
⚡
Speed is the entire game
80–85 seconds per question. No calculator at all. Mental math, approximation, and elimination are your tools. Drill timed mocks daily — speed is a skill, not a trait.
🏆
Go for the bonus 12
12 bonus questions with no negative marking = up to +36 marks. That's the difference between EEE Goa and EEE Hyderabad. Aim to finish 130 questions with 15+ min to spare.
📖
English + Reasoning = free 90 marks
Most JEE students ignore these sections and lose 20–30 marks. Vocabulary, synonyms/antonyms, grammar, analogies, number series — 2 weeks of daily practice locks most of this in.
⚠️
Respect the −1 penalty
Only guess when you've eliminated 2+ options. Random guess math: 75% wrong × −1 = −0.75, 25% right × +3 = +0.75 → expected value = 0. With 2 options eliminated: EV turns positive. Guess only then.
⏱️
90-second hard rule
Not solved in 90 sec? Mark and move. Never lose 3 easy questions chasing one stubborn hard one. You can revisit freely — no sectional lock.
📚
BITSAT Maths ≠ JEE Maths style
Coordinate Geometry (Circles, Straight Lines) has the highest single-chapter weightage in BITSAT Maths — far more than JEE. Recalibrate. Use BITSAT-specific question banks, not just JEE DPPs.
MSc Strategy: Branch Change is Real
MSc Physics/Math/Semiconductors → BITS campus entry → branch change after Semester 1 based on CGPA. Students regularly move from MSc to EEE/ECE this way. So even at 255, getting into Pilani MSc is a legitimate route to engineering branches.

The MSc → Branch Change Strategy
Get into any MSc at BITS. After Semester 1, if CGPA qualifies (~8.5+), you can apply for a dual degree in an engineering branch. EEE and ECE are the most accessible via this route. 🖥️ On-Screen Scientific Calculator: The COMEDK CBT interface provides a virtual scientific calculator for Physics/Maths computations. Physical calculators are strictly banned but you can use the on-screen one. Use it for complex calculations — don't waste time on mental arithmetic when the tool is right there.

Tiebreaker (4-stage hierarchy)
When scores are equal: (1) Higher Maths score → (2) Higher Physics score → (3) Higher Maths + Physics combined → (4) Older by date of birth. Maths is the most critical tiebreaker — always maximise Maths first.

Perfect Balance — But Maths Decides Ranks
Equal 60-60-60 split. No English, no Reasoning. Pure PCM. Maths is the tiebreaker at Stage 1 — when scores tie (very common at 130–140), Maths marks separate you. Invest extra time in Maths. Use the on-screen calculator for Physics numericals to save time.
Strategy
📖
NCERT is your bible
COMEDK is heavily CBSE Class 11 & 12 based. Every NCERT concept, example, and exercise is fair game. Master NCERT before going anywhere else — it covers 80%+ of what's asked.
🖥️
Use the on-screen calculator smartly
The virtual calculator is your edge. For multi-step Physics/Maths numericals, use it for final computation. Don't waste 30 seconds on mental arithmetic when the calc saves you in 5 seconds.
✅
Attempt every single question
Zero negative marking = zero downside to guessing. Finish all sections and return to blanks. Every unanswered question is a free mark you're leaving on the table.
📐
Maths = tiebreaker, so dominate it
In tied scores, Maths separates ranks. Score 50+/60 in Maths to cut through the crowd. Use your JEE calc prep — COMEDK Maths is significantly easier than JEE.
🔁
Switch subjects freely — no time limits
Unlike BITSAT or MHT CET, COMEDK has no sectional locks. If you're stuck in Physics, jump to Maths, finish that, then return. Maximise your total score tactically.
📝
Do 5+ COMEDK past papers
COMEDK repeats topic patterns very predictably. Past papers from 2020–2025 will prep you better than any coaching material. The format is far more predictable than JEE.
Your JEE 87th %ile → COMEDK
COMEDK is much easier than JEE. You should score 130–150+ comfortably. The risk: overconfidence → silly errors. 2 weeks of NCERT revision + 5 full mocks = MSRIT CSE / RVCE ECE range. Also remember: Maths tiebreaker matters a lot at ~130–145 band where many cluster.

Maharashtra vs All India Quota
Maharashtra domicile: Compete for 85% seats (State quota). Non-Maharashtra: Only 15% seats (AI quota) — cutoffs are 5–10 marks higher for AI quota. Knowing which category you fall in significantly changes your target score.

Maths = Half Your Score
100 out of 200 marks at +2 per question. Every extra Maths question correct = 2 marks. To go from 140→155, fix Maths first — getting 7–8 more Maths questions right = +14–16 marks. Physics and Chemistry are +1 each. Always prioritise Maths drilling.
Strategy
📐
Maths first — always
100/200 marks. Each question = 2 marks. Get 5 extra Maths right = +10 marks = the same as getting 10 more Physics correct. Maximise Maths before anything else.
⏰
Paper 1 timing is a trap
Phy+Chem locks after 90 min. Move quickly at ~54 sec/question. Don't drift into Maths-style deep thinking during Paper 1. Get it done, then give full 90 min to Maths (+2 per question).
✅
Attempt every question
No negative marking. Blank = 0. A guess = 25% chance of +1 or +2. Eliminate what you can and pick. Never leave blank.
📗
Fill Maharashtra Board gaps NOW
MHT CET uses the Maharashtra State Board syllabus. Chapters like Linear Programming, Mathematical Logic, Circular Motion, Wave Motion, Halogen Derivatives — these aren't in NCERT/JEE. Get state board books for these specifically (see Topics tab).
📝
Practice actual MHT CET PYQs
Question style differs from JEE — more formula-application, fewer derivations. 5 full MHT CET papers before exam day. The style shift from JEE is real — don't skip this calibration.
🏃
Speed in Phy+Chem (54 sec/question)
1-mark questions. Move fast. Don't spend 2 min on a 1-mark Physics question when Maths questions are worth double. Single-mark questions get ~40 seconds max; move on.
To reach COEP ECE (155+ marks)
You need: Maths 78+/100 · Physics 40+/50 · Chemistry 37+/50. Your JEE base gets you ~65–70 in Maths and ~75/100 combined in Phy+Chem. Closing the gap = state board chapters + mocks + more Maths drilling.
